## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Author the assembly playbook the product spec has promised since day one: the written play for Day 1's few-shot session. It scripts how a builder takes sponsor material (docs, SDKs, temp credentials, a Figma brief) plus a chosen core demo moment, and drives a high-agency coding agent on the deployed foundation into one tested end-to-end vertical slice — deciding in order what to prove, what to check with the harness, and what to defer — then converts every deferral into a thin, Vend-ready demand signal on the new project's own board so Lisa's RDSPI loop starts only when work is substantial enough to earn it. This matters now because the foundation pieces (deployed surface, harness, backstage, self-labeling frame) exist or are on the board, but nothing tells a person under event pressure how to spend their best one or two contexts across them; without the playbook, the template is parts without the assembly instructions its name promises.

## Value to the design

This is the charter's admission test 'improves the golden few-shot context before Lisa's heavier RDSPI loop is worthwhile' made into a shippable artifact. It advances P1 by front-loading the path from repo creation to a public, provable slice before ideation deepens, and P2 by making 'tested code' the playbook's mandatory exit condition — the session ends at an observably working core moment exercised under the harness's time budgets, not at plausible-looking code. As a permanent, every demo assembled from the template inherits the practiced sequence instead of re-improvising it per event.

## Done looks like

A dry run against a sample sponsor input proves the playbook end to end: starting from a freshly generated project and following only the playbook, one few-shot session yields a core-moment vertical slice live at the public URL with its checks passing, and the session's leftovers appear as one-line signals on that project's demand board — with the playbook itself readable in one sitting by the template author's target user.

## Context & constraints

Go-and-see: docs/knowledge already holds the raw material — charter, product-spec (which names the assembly playbook as a Prepare-phase deliverable), vend-workflow, rdspi-workflow, deployment, integration-check, backstage-retrieval-seam — so this play composes existing knowledge into one operational sequence rather than writing new doctrine. Prerequisites, named as such: the deployed foundation (E-001) and integration harness (E-002) are what the playbook drives and are still open on the board — and the project snapshot currently lists no src/** modules, so the dry-run leg depends on those epics landing, not on scope hidden here. Boundaries: per N2, the playbook prescribes seams and sequence, not an all-provider recipe library — provider-specific recipes wait for repeated evidence; per N4, it directs checks at hangs and regressions while leaving 'is the demo convincing' to human judgment; per the template-leak guardrail, the playbook ships as template knowledge but this repo's demand history and planning artifacts stay out of generated projects. Out of scope: Day-2 productization paths (demand signal #2, its own epic), handoff rehearsal (signal #3), and any automation or tooling around the playbook. Right-size (PE-7): one authored playbook plus one rehearsal pass fits a ~2-hour block; if the rehearsal exposes missing provider recipes or tooling, those return to the demand board as new signals rather than growing this card.
