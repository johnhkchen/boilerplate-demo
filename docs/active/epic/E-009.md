## Intent — the bigger-picture play

E-005 made the homepage self-labeling in the house 'parlor, not portfolio' voice, but the fix didn't hold: backstage drifted back to insider, self-referencing, over-long copy (a two-paragraph passcode lecture) because nothing binds new copy to the standard — each surface re-earns legibility by hand and quietly regresses. The bigger-picture play is to stop treating voice as a one-off cleanup and make it a durable, machine-readable contract: a short binding length/voice standard (plain kitchen-table English, brief, half-second legible, names as wayfinding, verb-forward labels) that the RDSPI loop reads at authoring time the way it already ingests the workflow doc — and to prove the standard by bringing the currently-drifted surfaces back to it in one corrective pass. Because the template's surfaces are inherited by every generated demo, a standard that travels with the loop means each project starts legible and stays legible instead of drifting to a wall of text the first time someone adds a paragraph.

## Value to the design

This realizes P3's legibility clause as a durable property rather than a snapshot — the public surface stays scannable on a projector and usable on a phone without a narrator — and lowers P4's front-door collaboration tax by keeping the stakeholder-facing copy in plain, account-free, kitchen-table language. As a permanent it converts stale, hand-maintained voice discipline into a portable contract the loop enforces, so the fix compounds across every demo the template generates instead of being re-applied and re-lost per surface.

## Done looks like

A person who has never seen the project reads every user-facing surface cold — once at projector distance, once on a phone — and finds each string brief, plain, and legible in about half a second, with no two-paragraph lecture, insider self-reference, or descriptive-sentence title anywhere; and a binding voice/length standard exists in the repo that the RDSPI loop reads at authoring time, demonstrated by the fact that copy exceeding it (as backstage did) is now held to the standard rather than shipping. The main Playwright flow still passes against the rewritten surfaces.

## Context & constraints

Go-and-see: src/pages/index.astro carries the E-005 self-labeling voice; src/pages/backstage.astro has regressed — its intro `.note` ('That passcode is a shared knock, not a vault…') and `.section-copy` paragraphs are the over-long insider lecture the signal names, and its `title`/`description` meta are candidates too; BaseLayout and any other user-facing strings are in scope for the sweep. The house voice already exists and is binding (the brand 'parlor, not portfolio' rules and b28-clay kit) — this epic does not invent a voice, it makes the existing one enforceable. Prerequisite (named as such, not hidden scope): the loop must be able to ingest a KB doc at authoring time; docs/knowledge/rdspi-workflow.md is already injected into agent context automatically per CLAUDE.md, so the standard rides the same seam — no new machinery, but the epic depends on that injection path existing. Boundaries/non-goals: template placeholder copy with obvious slots only — a generated project's real name and primary action come from its idea (N1); no CMS or content tooling enters (N5); the standard is a portable in-repo contract read by the loop, not a mandatory SaaS control plane (N3); an automated length/voice check may assert copy fits the envelope but must not claim the copy is convincing or replace human judgment (N4); no passcode, secret, or team-internal detail may surface on public copy, and template-development history must not leak into generated projects (P3, template-leak guardrail). Out of scope: restyling the clay kit, per-demo copywriting, and any change to backstage auth/storage. Right-size (PE-7): a short standard doc + wiring it into the loop's read path + one corrective sweep of the drifted surfaces and a projector/phone pass fits one ~2-hour block; if a later signal asks to enforce voice via an automated gate across the whole fleet, that is a separate epic, not hidden scope here.
