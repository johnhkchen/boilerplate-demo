## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Make the template's public surface introduce itself. Today the index page proves the wiring (the signed-note card) and backstage accepts stakeholder context, but the page still reads maker-outward: the visitor is told about the starting line rather than shown what to do with it, and nothing on the front page points a stakeholder toward the one thing they can actually do. This play recomposes the shipped surface so every element carries a verb-forward label ('Watch the server answer', 'Leave a note for the team'), the page exposes exactly one obvious primary action, and the stakeholder path to backstage is discoverable from the front door — so the demo works as a demo even when its author isn't in the room. Because the surface is the template's, every generated demo inherits the self-labeling frame with clear slots for its own name and primary action. (Clears demand.md signal #5.)

## Value to the design

This is P3's legibility clause made real — the page is scannable on a projector and actionable on a phone without a narrator — and it lowers P4's collaboration tax at the front door: a stakeholder finds the 'leave context' path from the public page instead of needing a link pasted in chat. As a permanent, the frame (name, one action, verb-forward section labels) is inherited by every demo the template generates, so each project starts self-explaining rather than re-earning legibility.

## Done looks like

Someone who has never seen the project opens the demo URL cold — once at projector distance, once on a phone — and can state unprompted what the page is, what its one primary action is, and how a visitor would leave a note for the team; no element on the page requires the author's explanation, and the main Playwright flow still passes against the recomposed surface.

## Context & constraints

Go-and-see: src/pages/index.astro ships a working two-card surface (identity card + live signed-note card) and src/pages/backstage.astro is the shared-passcode stakeholder surface, but index copy is descriptive rather than verb-forward, there is no single primary action, and backstage is unreachable from the front page. The house voice and kit already exist and are binding: b28-clay tokens/primitives and the 'parlor, not portfolio' brand rules (verb-forward labels, names as wayfinding) — this epic applies that standard to the template surface, not a new design system. Boundaries: template placeholder copy with obvious slots only — the generated project's real name and primary action come from the idea (N1), and no CMS or content tooling enters for this (N5); a Playwright assertion may check the labeled action exists and responds, but does not claim the page is convincing (N4); backstage stays passcode-gated and no secret or team-internal detail may surface on the public page (P3, template-leak guardrail). No prerequisites — all touched surfaces exist and are live. Out of scope: restyling the clay kit itself, per-demo copywriting, and any change to backstage's auth or storage. Right-size (PE-7): copy/label recomposition plus one primary-action affordance and a projector/phone pass fits one ~2-hour block.
