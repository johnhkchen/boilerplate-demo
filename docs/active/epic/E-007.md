## Intent — the bigger-picture play

Prove P6 sovereignty is real by dress-rehearsing a full transfer of a live demo to a second owner, and capture what the rehearsal reveals as a reusable handoff runbook. Today the template asserts projects are 'independently ownable, operable, and transferable,' but that claim has never been exercised end to end — so the seams that would break on a real transfer (a domain still pointed at the author's zone, a Cloudflare resource bound to the author's account, a secret that was never rotatable, a check that only passes on the author's machine) stay invisible until a handoff is actually needed and it is too late. The play is a drill: hand the whole surface — code, Cloudflare resources, domains, data, configuration, checks, and rotated secrets — to a fresh owner, watch what fails, and turn the failures into a runbook and a gap list the fleet reuses. Intent only; DecomposeEpic breaks this into the per-category transfer steps and the drill harness.

## Value to the design

Realizes verifiable transferability: it converts P6/P5's stated sovereignty from an aspiration into observed evidence, and produces a reusable runbook so every future demo can be handed off the same way rather than each transfer being reinvented. This directly advances P6 (independently ownable, operable, transferable without the author's central services) and P5's promise that a proven demo transfers to a new owner without a rewrite.

## Done looks like

A second person, following the transfer runbook against a real demo, takes ownership of its repo, Cloudflare resources, domain, data, and configuration, rotates every secret out of the author's control, and the demo still passes its own checks under the new owner — with the author's accounts and any central/fleet services removed from the runtime path. Any category that could not be cleanly transferred is named explicitly as a known gap rather than silently skipped.

## Context & constraints

Grounds against a template repo whose deployment, domain, secret-management, and check surfaces are established by prior epics (E-001..E-006); a deployed demo with those seams is a PREREQUISITE for a meaningful rehearsal, not part of this epic's scope. Respect N3 and P7: the handoff must work WITHOUT a mandatory SaaS control plane or fleet service — this epic builds a portable runbook and a drill, not a central 'handoff-as-a-service.' Respect N2: transfer is exercised over the Cloudflare-first surface the template already uses; a general all-provider transfer abstraction is out of scope. Respect the guardrail that template history/demand must not leak into generated projects — the runbook is a project artifact, not template internals. Right-sizing (PE-7): this epic is the rehearsal-plus-runbook-plus-gap-list drill, sized to ~one 2-hour block; fully automating clean transfer of every category (data migration, secret rotation tooling, domain re-delegation) is plausibly SEVERAL follow-on epics and should be minted separately from the gaps this drill surfaces, not inflated into this card.
